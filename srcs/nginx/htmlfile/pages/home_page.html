What is TRONPONG?

TRONPONG is a futuristic reimagining of the classic Pong game, set in the neon-lit world of TRON. It combines the simplicity of Pong with the high-energy, digital aesthetic of the TRON universe.

Players control light-cycle-inspired paddles, battling it out in a grid-like arena where every move leaves a trail of light. Experience the thrill of classic gameplay with a cyber-enhanced twist!

What is Pong?

Pong is one of the earliest arcade video games and a classic of the medium. It simulates table tennis, featuring simple two-dimensional graphics. Players control paddles to hit a ball back and forth across the screen.

The game's simplicity and intuitive gameplay made it a hit and helped establish the video game industry. Pong's influence can still be seen in many modern games, including our own TRONPONG!

The History of Pong

1972: The Birth of Pong

Pong was created by Allan Alcorn and released by Atari in 1972. It was one of the first video games to reach mainstream popularity and helped to establish the video game industry.

1975: Home Pong

Atari released Home Pong, bringing the arcade experience into living rooms across America. This move helped to kickstart the home console market.

1976-Present: Legacy and Influence

Pong's success inspired numerous clones and variations. Its simple yet addictive gameplay continues to influence game design to this day, including modern reimaginings like TRONPONG.
